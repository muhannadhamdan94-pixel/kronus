The problem with other calculators

They tell you how long.
Not how to finish faster.

Every other debt calculator starts from minimum payments and works forward. It shows you a payoff date years away, assumes you keep paying the same amount forever, and gives you nothing to act on.

Kronus starts from your discipline. It takes your full available surplus each month and directs it entirely at one debt until that debt is gone. When it closes, that freed payment stacks into the next one. The speed compounds every time you win.

Your savings are protected from the start. Your fixed bills are accounted for. What remains is your payoff weapon, and Kronus shows you exactly what it can do.

The question is not how long minimum payments take. The question is: with real discipline, how fast can I actually be free?

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know.

Most calculators take your minimum payments and show you how long your debt will last. Kronus does the opposite. It takes your full available surplus each month and directs it entirely at one debt until that debt closes. When it closes, the freed payment stacks into the next one. The result is the fastest mathematically possible payoff path built from your real numbers, not a worst-case minimum-payment projection.
Kronus subtracts your fixed bills and protected savings from your monthly income to find your available pool. Each month, that full pool attacks your current target debt. When it closes, the freed payment rolls into the next debt automatically. This gives you a closing date for every debt in the sequence, not just the last one.
The snowball method pays off your smallest balance first while making minimum payments on all others. Once the smallest closes, its payment rolls into the next smallest, building momentum. Research consistently shows that the snowball method helps people stay motivated and stick to their plan, which matters more than mathematical optimisation in the short term.
The avalanche method attacks your highest interest rate debt first. It saves the most money in total interest over time. Kronus lets you toggle between snowball and avalanche and shows the difference in months and total interest paid side by side, so you can choose with full information rather than a guess.
Yes, and this is a core feature. You set a monthly savings amount and it is ring-fenced before any debt calculation runs. It is never used for payments, even in months where the pool runs tight. Your savings balance grows every month alongside your shrinking debt, so you can see both at the same time.
You decide how to classify them. Mark a debt as a fixed bill and Kronus pays its minimum every month while your closing queue runs. Once all closeable debts are cleared, Kronus enters Phase 2 and your full surplus pivots to attack fixed bill debts in the order you choose, with a closing date for each one.

What we store

When you create a Kronus account, we store your email address and an encrypted password. That is all. We use Supabase as our database provider, hosted on secure infrastructure. Your password is never stored in plain text — it is hashed before it reaches our database.

What we never store

Every number you enter into the Kronus sequencer — your income, debts, bills, savings amounts, interest rates, or any simulation result — runs entirely inside your browser. None of it is transmitted to our servers. None of it is stored anywhere. When you close the tab, it is gone. We have no access to your financial information and we never will.

Third-party services

Kronus uses Supabase for authentication only. We do not use advertising networks, analytics trackers, or data brokers. We do not sell your information to any third party. We do not share your email address with anyone.

Deleting your account

You can delete your account at any time from inside the tool. When you delete your account, your email address and password hash are permanently removed from our database within 24 hours. There is nothing else to delete because we never stored anything else.
